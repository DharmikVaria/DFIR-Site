Practice Layer

DFIR Labs and TryHackMe Practice Rooms

Practical labs help convert theory into workflow confidence. The aim is not only to get flags, but to practice evidence handling, artifact interpretation, timeline construction, and reportable reasoning.

How to use these labs properly

Treat each lab like a small case. Record the question, evidence source, tools used, commands, timestamps, findings, screenshots, and conclusion. After solving, write a short investigation note: what happened, what evidence supports it, what remains uncertain, and what artifact was most valuable.

Beginners often chase answers without understanding artifacts. A better approach is to build a repeatable workflow: identify evidence, preserve context, parse artifacts, correlate timestamps, and explain findings.
